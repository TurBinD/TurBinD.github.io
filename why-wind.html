Why Use Wind Energy?

 

Clean and Renewable

Wind is a constant daily occurrence from solar radiation. As long as the sun is burring we will have wind. This makes it a reliable renewable energy source. Wind energy doesn't pollute. There is no air, soil, or water pollution, no burning of fuels to release CO2, no toxic chemicals and no radioactive waste. It simply uses wind passing over a few large blades to generate power.

 

Cost Effective

Wind energy produces power at an equivalent cost per kilowatt as traditional power plants. Unlike the fuel used in power plants, the cost of wind will never increase. Wind is free and the only costs associated with wind is the turbines and the power grid. Wind energy does not contribute to resource scarcity or climate change meaning its cost will not be affected by future carbon taxes.

 

Easy Installation

Building a wind turbine is much simpler than any other type of power plant, and anyone can have one. A large scale wind farm can be built and operational in less than a year when many other power plants would take years to complete. A standard utility turbine is  a very tall pole with a generator and a large set of blades. This is a very simple construction and allows for rapid construction to finish projects in a short time. Since a wind turbine can come in any size anyone can put up a turbine. Small scale residential turbines can be constructed in less than a week.

 

Good for Rural Areas

For rural communities, wind energy is a low cost alternative power lines connecting the communities to power grids from larger urban areas. Wind energy can also be used in conjunction with rural land use. Wind farms do not require much ground space allowing for farmers to use the land for crops of grazing animals. Having Wind turbines in rural areas increases the need for skilled technicians for maintenance, diversifying the economy.

 

Scalable

Wind energy systems are available in all shapes and sizes. Two main types of turbines are horizontal axis wind turbines (HAWT) and vertical axis wind turbines (VAWT). These can be broken down even further into small scale and utility scale. Small scale turbines are for a residential personal level that allows anyone to have a wind energy system. The utility scale turbines the turbines used for large scale energy production. Since there are many different wind energy systems, and many different ways to apply them, wind energy is very scalable to meet any power need no matter how small or how large.
