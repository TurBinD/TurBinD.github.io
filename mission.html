Our Mission

 

Design and build a vertical axis wind turbine for a phone charging station that will raise awareness and promote the use of small scale wind energy systems.

 

The University of Maryland spends tens of millions of dollars, and uses hundreds of gigawatt hours of power, every year from unsustainable energy sources. Unsustainable energy is a hot topic and around the world a large push for sustainable energy has begun. The university has set goals to become carbon independent by the year 2050 and currently has many off-campus sustainable energy sites providing the University power. Our team set out to help increase the visibility of sustainable energy on campus through the use of small scale wind turbines. We have designed and built a vertical axis wind turbine to be used as a phone charging station on campus. We aim to increase the awareness of small scale wind systems by using an eye-catching turbine in visible areas on campus that will encourage students and visitors to research wind energy and learn more about small scale wind turbines and their applications and benefits. We hope that through our efforts we can promote the carbon independent energy already used on campus and educate the community on small scale wind energy systems.
